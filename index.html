< THE INVOLUNTARY RECOMBINATOR >


Anagram Sequence Analyzer v1.0




> WHAT IS THIS TERMINAL USED FOR?

The Involuntary Recombinator sequence analyzer is a tool meant to aid in discovering, developing, and checking the accuracy of anagrams.


> HOW DO I USE THIS TOOL?

Input a "seed phrase" into the initial field, and your attempted anagram in the field below it. The tool will check the two phrases and isolate which letters are left over, and which do not match. You can use the leftover letters to structure new words and complete the anagram.

For instance, the phrase "the sacred soul is eternal" is a complex item to make an anagram for. However, looking at the available letters, we can extract a few words like "a", "sad", "lost", and "cult".

If we wanted to make a grammatically significant anagram, we could start with what we have extracted to form a partial sequence: "a sad lost cult". By inputting this into the second field, the analyzer will tell us that the letters [ h, e, r, e, i, s, e, e, r, n ] are still left over. Can you figure out a way to use those remaining 10 letters to finish the sentence?


> HOW DOES THE TOOL WORK?

This tool works very much like how you would do this yourself with a pen and paper, but it automates the tedious parts. If you were doing this by hand, you would have to write out the initial phrase and the attempted anagram, crossing off letters individually to see what was left over. Every time you came up with a new attempt, you would have to rewrite everything and repeat the process. When it comes to making anagrams that are 20+ letters long across multiple words, this becomes a particularly mentally taxing and slow process.

This analyzer streamlines that by taking your seed phrase and attempt, placing the letters from each into their own "buckets," and then running through them to check off each character one by one. Depending on what doesn't add up at the end, the tool prints a simple notice letting you know if there are too many letters, not enough letters, or a complete mismatch, and outputs exactly which characters remain.


> WHY WAS THIS ENGINEERED?

I created this tool to help me find anagrams for a sci-fi novel I have toyed with writing. The story's working title is "The Involuntary Recombinator," and it heavily involves the recombination of words, letters, and matter as part of its broader narrative.

Initially, I used LLMs (Large Language Models) to try and help me develop complex anagrams. I had several strong "aha!" moments, only to find out later that what the AI assured me were perfect anagrams were actually off by a couple of letters. The AI would hype me up, but the math was wrong.

After doing some testing and research, I learned that this exact type of task—counting all the characters in a sequence and verifying their presence in another—is something LLMs are inherently terrible at. This is due to "tokenization," where the AI breaks words down into chunks rather than individual letters, making it terrible at storing and comparing single characters in isolation. While AI is an extremely useful tool for many things, it is much more effective to use a simple, deterministic program like the one on this page for strict character validation.


> WHAT IS "THE INVOLUNTARY RECOMBINATOR" ABOUT?

It is still a work in progress with more lore than narrative at this point. In the distant future of our universe, the Involuntary Recombinator is a spiritual Mecca where many alien species gather for spiritual growth. The Recombinator is a massive structure able to disassemble, combine, splice, and restructure matter and information at its most fundamental levels.

In the present day, beings voluntarily submit themselves to the Recombinator for spiritual reasons—often to combine with dying loved ones rather than letting them pass away separately.

However, as the spiritual hub undergoes deeper scrutiny, a group of individuals begins to realize it has mysterious and daunting origins. There may very well be a connection between this new spiritual Mecca and an unstoppable, ancient superstructure that once wreaked havoc on the universe by ingesting entire galaxies and combining them without consent.

Perhaps there is more than meets the eye? Take the phrase "THE INVOLUNTARY RECOMBINATOR" and the ancient machine known as the "VRN ROBOTIC UNITER ANOMALY". Run them through this analyzer, and you may realize that these two disparate entities are far more connected than the universe ever thought!
